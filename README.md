# TITLE OF THE PRODUCT AUTOMOBILE PARTS PRODUCTION FACTORY

# DESCRIPTION
The factory is designed for the mass production of automotive components such as brake discs, brake calipers, steering knuckles, transmission shafts, wheel hubs, and suspension brackets. The facility integrates machining, assembly, quality inspection, warehousing, and logistics operations within a digitally simulated manufacturing environment using DELMIA.
# CHALLENGES FACED TO DEVELOP
Creating an automobile parts production factory simulation in DELMIA involves several challenges. One of the primary difficulties is designing an accurate factory layout that efficiently accommodates CNC machines, robots, conveyors, storage areas, and assembly stations while ensuring smooth material flow and safe operator movement. Developing detailed 3D models of manufacturing resources and importing them from CAD software can be time-consuming and may affect simulation performance due to large file sizes. Another significant challenge is collecting and defining realistic process data, including machining times, setup times, inspection durations, transportation delays, and machine downtime, as inaccurate data can lead to unrealistic simulation results. Material handling systems such as forklifts, conveyors, and AGVs must be carefully programmed to prevent congestion and optimize workflow throughout the factory. Robot simulation also requires accurate path planning and collision detection to ensure efficient and safe operation. Additionally, balancing production lines, identifying bottlenecks, and optimizing resource utilization are essential tasks that require extensive analysis. Human factors must also be considered by evaluating operator ergonomics, workstation accessibility, and safety conditions. Furthermore, integrating data from various software platforms, maintaining compatibility between models, and validating simulation results against real-world manufacturing conditions can be complex. Finally, large-scale factory simulations demand substantial computational resources, making model optimization and performance management critical for achieving accurate and efficient results.
